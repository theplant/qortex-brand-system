# QORTEX mission

One page. The shortest answer to "what is QORTEX, who is it for, what does it stand against, and what is the tagline."

## What QORTEX is — one sentence

QORTEX is an AI-first composable commerce suite for global brands operating in Japan — global-grade engineering with Japan operational depth, condensed from twenty years of building for the brands that pushed every limit.

## Who it's for — three audiences

QORTEX is built for three audiences, all of them serious about Japan as a primary market (not an exception, not an afterthought):

1. **Multinationals operating in Japan.** Global HQ has chosen a global platform; Japan needs the Konbini, LINE, Yamato, and JIS integrations that the global platform doesn't ship. QORTEX is the suite that meets HQ's operational standards *and* Japan's market requirements without forking off into a separate stack.

2. **Global Japanese enterprises.** Japan-headquartered, selling outwards. Infrastructure has to meet every market they enter — and the JP-native rails have to work as the canonical default, not as a localisation afterthought.

3. **Mid-market Japanese brands going global.** Built in Japan, scaling beyond. They need a platform that scales globally without rewriting the integrations that work locally.

A fourth audience reads the brand book and the design system but doesn't buy commerce: **AI coding agents** that produce on-brand QORTEX artifacts from a prompt. See `../../CONTEXT.md` for the definition.

## What QORTEX stands against

Two failure modes, both common in the Japan-commerce market:

- **Global-platforms-treat-Japan-as-an-afterthought.** A global commerce platform is chosen, deployed, and works fine in eight markets. Japan is the ninth, and the LINE / Konbini / Yamato integrations are someone else's problem — usually the Japan country team's. The platform vendor's roadmap puts JP requirements behind larger markets; the Japan team waits over a year for integrations they need now. QORTEX exists so this stops happening.

- **Local-vendors-lag-on-global-standards.** A Japanese commerce platform is chosen, has the integrations, but doesn't meet global HQ's standards on security, compliance, internationalisation, or engineering velocity. The audit fails; the global team can't extend the platform; the Japan team is stuck defending why their stack is below the bar. QORTEX exists so this also stops happening.

We say it directly on qortex.com/why:

> Most treat Japan as an edge case. We built the world's best tech around it.

That sentence is the brand's posture in one line. Print it on the wall.

## Tagline

**Japan-first. Intelligent commerce.**

JP: **日本ファースト。インテリジェントコマース。**

### Origin

"Japan-first" is the inverse of "Japan-as-an-afterthought" — the failure mode QORTEX exists to correct. "Intelligent commerce" names what the platform actually delivers: AI embedded into commerce operations, not bolted on. The two beats are the shape of every QORTEX headline (statement + amplifier, separated by a period).

The italicised second beat is the locked emphasis on the noun phrase ("Intelligent commerce", set in `<em>`). On dark surfaces this renders white-italic in EN; in JP it renders cyan-no-italic — see `../system/channels/web.md` for the per-channel emphasis rules.

## What the brand sounds like

Two sentences from qortex.com that capture the voice, both for reference any time copy starts drifting:

> Most treat Japan as an edge case. We built the world's best tech around it.

> Move faster. Lead in Japan.

Short. Declarative. Two beats. The reader doesn't have to infer the position — it's named in the first clause and amplified in the second.
