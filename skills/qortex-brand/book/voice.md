# QORTEX voice

Voice is encoded as **specimens + anti-specimens**, not as adjective lists. Pattern-match against the real QORTEX sentences below before generating copy.

## How to use this file

Six sections cover the common copy moves: **headlines**, **subheads**, **paragraphs**, **CTAs**, **eyebrows**, and **microcopy**. Each section has:

- **Specimens** — real QORTEX sentences captured verbatim from qortex.com (the home page, /why, /jp, etc). Do not paraphrase them; pattern-match against them.
- **Anti-specimens** — written to sound like generic enterprise SaaS copy that a QORTEX draft must *not* drift toward. They are the strawman — if your draft sounds closer to the anti-specimen than the specimen, rewrite.
- A short rule that names the contrast.

Common moves you'll see across the specimens:

- **Two beats per line.** A declarative statement, then a counterpoint or amplifier, separated by a period. "Japan-first. Intelligent commerce." / "Move faster. Lead in Japan."
- **Concrete nouns over abstract gerunds.** "commerce", "team", "brands", "Konbini", "LINE" — not "solutions", "experiences", "ecosystems".
- **One adjective max. Often zero.**
- **The emphasised phrase italicises a noun**, not a verb. "Japan-first. _Intelligent commerce._"
- **Specific proper nouns are encouraged.** LINE, PayPay, Yamato, Konbini. These earn credibility every time they appear.

## Headlines

### Specimens

1. Japan-first. _Intelligent commerce._
2. AI embedded where your team already works.
3. Designed together. _Shipped fast._
4. Move faster. Lead in Japan.
5. Japan isn't an afterthought. It's our primary market.

### Anti-specimens

1. Revolutionize your commerce with our cutting-edge AI-powered enterprise solution for the Japanese market.
2. Empowering teams to unlock seamless growth through next-generation intelligent automation.
3. We built a comprehensive platform that delivers innovative results, faster.
4. Transform your customer journey with our holistic, end-to-end commerce experience.
5. The future of intelligent commerce is here.

The contrast: every specimen is under 8 words and uses concrete nouns ("commerce", "team", "Japan"). Every anti-specimen leans on AI-marketing filler ("revolutionize", "cutting-edge", "seamless", "next-generation", "comprehensive", "holistic"). If your draft contains any of those filler words, delete the sentence and start again.

## Subheads

A subhead sits under a headline and does one job: explain what the noun in the headline actually is. Single sentence. 12–22 words.

### Specimens

1. An AI-first composable commerce suite for global brands operating in Japan.
2. Global-standard composable architecture, Japan operational depth, enterprise-scale performance — all built in.
3. AI embedded where your team already works.
4. 80% pre-built core. 20% tailored to your operations.
5. One data model across all modules means AI actually works.

### Anti-specimens

1. The leading enterprise-grade commerce platform for organisations seeking to expand their digital presence into the Japanese market with confidence.
2. Our innovative composable architecture combined with deep market expertise empowers your team to deliver world-class customer experiences.
3. A unified solution that brings together all the tools and integrations your business needs to succeed.
4. Powered by AI, designed for scale, built for the modern enterprise.
5. Where strategy meets execution, and ambition meets ability.

The contrast: every specimen is one declarative sentence under 22 words that names *what the product is*. Every anti-specimen is an aspirational mood piece that names nothing — "world-class customer experiences" could be selling anything.

## Paragraphs

Body paragraphs (2–4 sentences each) sit under section H2s and explain the position with concrete framing. The QORTEX paragraph leans on **diagnosed problems**, not **claimed capabilities**.

### Specimens

1. Global platforms treat Japan as an afterthought. Local vendors often lag on global communication, compliance, security, and technology standards. QORTEX combines leading-edge MACH architecture with Japan operational depth, engineered for the volumes that break standard platforms.
2. It doesn't matter how good your agency is. When the delivery chain has multiple links (platform vendor, SI, local agency, internal IT), each one is a potential bottleneck.
3. Assembling your stack from best-of-breed vendors sounds logical on paper. What it doesn't account for is who owns the integration layer between them, and what happens to your data and AI ambitions when every capability depends on a different company.
4. One pattern we hear constantly: Japan teams waiting over a year for critical integrations (LINE, domestic payments, logistics) that never make it onto the global roadmap.
5. We start with your operations, not our product. Then we build together — and go live faster than you'd expect.

### Anti-specimens

1. At QORTEX, we believe in empowering businesses to achieve their full potential through innovative technology solutions that drive measurable outcomes and unlock sustainable growth.
2. Our platform leverages cutting-edge AI and machine learning to deliver personalised experiences at scale, transforming how leading enterprises engage with their customers.
3. We understand that every business is unique, which is why our flexible, scalable, modular architecture adapts to your needs — wherever you are on your digital journey.
4. By combining best-in-class technology with deep industry expertise, we help organisations navigate the complexities of modern commerce with confidence.
5. From day one, our commitment is to your success. We partner with you every step of the way to ensure that your business not only meets but exceeds its objectives.

The contrast: every specimen opens with a **specific problem someone is having** ("Global platforms treat Japan as an afterthought.", "Japan teams waiting over a year for…"). Every anti-specimen opens with a **claim about QORTEX** ("At QORTEX, we believe…", "Our platform leverages…"). If the first sentence of a paragraph is about QORTEX, rewrite it to be about the reader's situation.

## CTAs

Buttons. Short. Verb-first. Some end with a `→`. Capitalisation matches the body case (sentence case, not title case).

### Specimens

1. Book a personalised demo →
2. See how we deliver →
3. Download the overview
4. Download Introduction to QORTEX
5. Download now →

### Anti-specimens

1. Get Started
2. Click Here to Learn More About Our Solutions
3. Connect With Our Team Today
4. Explore the Possibilities
5. Discover the QORTEX Difference

The contrast: every specimen is a **specific action with a specific object** ("a personalised demo", "the overview", "Introduction to QORTEX"). Every anti-specimen is a **generic call** ("Get Started", "Learn More") that could appear on any SaaS button. If the button's label could be lifted onto a competitor's page without changing meaning, rewrite.

## Eyebrows

The small ALL-CAPS label above an H2. Names the section in the smallest possible noun phrase. Either a category ("The Solution", "The AI Layer") or a friction-naming question ("Tired of delays?", "Why are loyalty members churning after their first redemption?").

### Specimens

1. The Solution
2. The AI Layer
3. World-class tech stack
4. Japan-native integrations
5. Who we work with
6. Tired of delays?
7. Avoid the "Best of Breed" trap.

### Anti-specimens

1. Our Approach
2. Key Benefits
3. Why Choose Us
4. About QORTEX
5. Solutions Overview
6. The Future of Commerce
7. Strategic Imperatives

The contrast: every specimen is either a **concrete category** ("The AI Layer", "Japan-native integrations") or a **diagnosed friction** ("Tired of delays?", "Avoid the 'Best of Breed' trap."). Every anti-specimen is **page furniture** that says nothing ("Our Approach", "Key Benefits"). Eyebrows should earn their place; if you'd be fine removing the eyebrow, the eyebrow isn't doing work.

## Microcopy

Tiny text — nav items, form labels, footers, tooltip-style strings. Quiet, direct, no sales tone.

### Specimens

1. Home
2. Solutions
3. Differentiators
4. Delivery Model
5. No spam. One email with your PDF, nothing else.

### Anti-specimens

1. Explore Our Platform
2. Discover Our Solutions
3. Why QORTEX?
4. Learn About Our Approach
5. We respect your privacy and will never share your information with third parties.

The contrast: every specimen reads like product UI — one noun, no verb decoration ("Home", "Solutions"). The privacy-style microcopy is short, direct, and slightly playful ("No spam. One email with your PDF, nothing else."). Every anti-specimen pads the nav into an aspirational phrase ("Explore Our Platform") or pads the privacy line into a legal-template paragraph. If a nav item has more than one word and the second word is a verb or adjective, it's wrong.

## JP voice — specimens

JP uses the same two-beat structure as EN, but signals emphasis via katakana for noun phrases (rather than italics) and ends declarative beats with `。`. Comma-driven cadence inside clauses uses `、`. JP-specific moves are tagged in the rules below the specimens.

### Headlines

1. 日本ファースト。<em>インテリジェントコマース。</em>
2. 共に設計。<em>素早く公開。</em>
3. 次の可能性を、ご一緒に。
4. チームが普段働く場所に、AI を組み込む。
5. 日本でもっとも要求水準の高いブランドに 20 年寄り添ってきた経験から。

### Headlines — anti-specimens

1. AI を活用した革新的なエンタープライズ商取引ソリューションで、お客様のビジネスを次のステージへ。
2. シームレスで包括的な体験を、業界最先端の技術で実現します。
3. 私たちが提供する次世代プラットフォームは、グローバル基準のイノベーションを日本市場へお届けします。
4. お客様の成功を、私たちの最優先事項として。
5. デジタルトランスフォーメーションの新時代を、ともに切り拓きましょう。

### Subheads

1. 日本で事業を展開するグローバルブランドのために設計された、AI ファーストでコンポーザブルなコマース。
2. 80% は構築済みのコア。20% は御社の業務に合わせてカスタマイズ。
3. 1 つのデータモデルがすべてのモジュールを貫くから、AI は本当に機能する。
4. LINE、アプリ、Web、店頭をまたいで顧客プロフィールを統合。1 つの ID で、すべてのタッチポイントを。
5. 開発者なしで、ページや季節キャンペーンを公開。ヘッドレス、API ファースト、高速。

### CTAs

1. パーソナライズドデモを依頼する →
2. 概要資料をダウンロード
3. 私たちの提供方法を見る →
4. 今すぐダウンロード →

### Microcopy

1. ホーム
2. 会社メールアドレス
3. 会社名
4. 以下に同意します
5. スパムは送信しません。PDF をお届けするメール 1 通のみです。

### JP-specific moves

- **Katakana for the emphasised noun phrase**, set in `<em>`. "<em>インテリジェントコマース。</em>", "<em>素早く公開。</em>". Avoid wrapping verbs in `<em>` — emphasis lands on the noun.
- **Comma-driven cadence**. The full-width `、` separates beats inside a clause; the full-width `。` ends a beat. Two-beat headlines use one `。` between the two parts.
- **No negative letter-spacing on display**. JP characters carry their own tracking — tightening them produces a broken-glyph effect. See `../system/channels/social.md` for the per-channel rule.
- **`word-break: keep-all`, `overflow-wrap: anywhere`**. Standard for any JP display surface so JP wraps on phrase boundaries while embedded English brand names are allowed to wrap anywhere.
- **Half-width English brand names embedded in JP** (LINE, QORTEX, MACH, AI) are preserved as-is. Surround with thin spacing as needed via the JS-native rendering — never substitute katakana for the brand name.
- **`お` and `ご` prefixes** are used where natural in business JP (御社, ご一緒する, お届け) but not piled on for politeness theatre. The QORTEX register is professional, not overly formal — closer to a senior consultant than a service-provider apologising for existing.

The same contrast principle applies: when a JP draft starts to feel like a press release ("お届けします" stacked across multiple sentences, "次世代", "新時代" filler), rewrite. The brand voice in JP is closer to a product caption than a corporate announcement.
