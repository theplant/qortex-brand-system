Context

200 stores, three legacy systems, one unhappy checkout.

Acme Co runs 200 specialty retail stores across Japan plus a 5-million-MAU e-commerce site. Before QORTEX, their commerce stack was a 12-year-old custom platform stitched to a global ERP, a local payments gateway, and a separate LINE bot — three systems, three databases, three places a customer's order could go wrong.

The checkout abandoned at 78%. Konbini orders failed reconciliation 1 in 4 times. LINE didn't talk to web. Customer support spent half its day translating between systems instead of helping customers.

Challenge

"We needed Konbini and LINE checkout in a quarter — not a year."

The board had given the team one buying season. The standard approach (replatform from scratch, run dual stacks for a year, migrate) was off the table. Acme's CFO wanted a number, the COO wanted a date, and the head of e-commerce wanted both without spending the next three quarters on integration debt.

Two requirements were non-negotiable:

  • Konbini reconciliation that doesn't drift. Every order paid at Lawson, FamilyMart, or Seven-Eleven had to settle without manual intervention.
  • One identity across LINE, web, and store. A customer who joined via LINE had to recognise their loyalty balance at the store register.
Solution

Three QORTEX modules. Pre-built integrations. One configured workflow.

We deployed Customer Identity, Order Management, and the Marketing Automation module — the smallest set that solved the two non-negotiables. The rest stayed in flight for phase two.

Customer Identity

Unified profile across LINE, web, and 200 store registers. JIS address validation, kanji ⇄ romaji on every form.

Order Management

Konbini, Pay-easy, COD pre-integrated. Reconciliation runs nightly against payment-gateway exports — no manual matching.

Marketing Automation

LINE Messaging API tied to the customer profile. Cart-abandonment and seasonal campaigns ship pre-templated.

Results

Eight weeks to live, +47% LINE conversion, zero reconciliation drift.

First production traffic ran in week eight. Konbini reconciliation has not required a manual match in the four months since launch. LINE checkout converts at 47% above web. Customer-support load dropped 31% in the first quarter.

Phase two — Personalisation, PIM, and Content Management — is in flight for Q3.
